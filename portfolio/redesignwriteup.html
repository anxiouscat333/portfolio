Responsive Redesign

Part I: Identifying Usability Problems

Chosen Interface: Kenyan Ministry of Health Website

Webpage Link: https://www.health.go.ke/


Why I chose this website: This website is supposed to be a reliable resource for public/social service but its poor design is rendering it inaccessible to a lot of people and thus defeating its own purpose.

Identified Usability Problems

  1. Small Font Sizes: Visitors could struggle to read the text on the website.
  2. Bad Navigation: Too many links all over the page. It makes the page feel cluttered and could confuse users.
  3. Too many moving components: The shifting carousel and moving components as you scroll down the page make it feel too busy.
  4. Obvious Developer Mistakes: A poor layout design that could make users not take the site seriously.

Accessibility Problems

WebAIM identified the following issues (summarized):

  • Linked Images Missing Alternative Texts
  • Very Low Contrast
  • Broken & Redundant Links
  • Very small text
  • Structural element issues
    •

I agree with the detected problems because even through visual observation I can confirm some of the identified issues e.g. I can see that some areas have low contrast between the text and background, the text is clearly too small in a lot of areas on the site, there doesn't seem to be a structural hierarchy that a user can use to identify potential workflows and/or get the desired information.
